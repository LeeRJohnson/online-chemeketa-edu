Pop-ups - More Information

Pop-up window blocking software is designed to prevent annoying pop-up ads when you are surfing the net) will treat those new windows as pop-ups and block them. If you click on Begin Quiz, or Compose Message and nothing happens, the cause may be a pop-up blocker. You will need to disable it in order to use these functions of eLearn. You can tell what software/programs are currently active by looking at the bottom right task bar. Move the mouse over the icons to see what they are. When you find one that is an antivirus, firewall, or pop-up ad blocker, right click to get to the properties. You should be able to disable just the pop-up ad blocking part but if not, there should be a way to turn off the firewall or whatever while you are working on eLearn (usually just a box that you tick to enable or disable). You can turn it back on when you are not working on eLearn.

Announcements, quizzes, discussions, mail, and chat all open up in a new window. Pop-up killer applications work in the background while you browse the internet. When they detect a pop-up window that may be an unwanted advertisement, they automatically close the window. This can prevent the Elearn system from performing properly.

To disable pop-up killers or ad-blockers:

  1. Windows XP with Service Pack 2: Internet Explorer - Tools menu - Pop-up Blocker: Either Turn Off Pop-up Blocker, or choose Pop-up Blocker settings to allow Pop-up windows on all Chemeketa.edu sites.
  2. Search your hard drive for any software with "pop-up" in the name and disable it. Examples of popular pop-up killer applications include:
    • Pop-Up Stopper
    • Pop-Up Defender
    • Pop-Up Zapper
  3. Pop-up killing or ad-blocking functions are included in some anti-virus, internet security, personal firewall, and browser toolbar programs. Disable the relevant setting in the program. Examples include:
    • Norton Security
    • McAfee
